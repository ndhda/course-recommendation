What is MCDM ?

Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM) is a process used to evaluate and prioritize multiple conflicting criteria. It helps in making decisions that involve various factors.

Essential in scenarios where decisions must balance different factors, making it widely applicable in diverse fields.


Applications of MCDM

Real-World Examples

Resource Allocation: Optimizing the use of limited resources.

Policy Making: Evaluating the impacts of different policy options.

Project Selection: Choosing the best projects based on multiple criteria.

Benefits of MCDM

Structured Decision Making: Provides a clear framework for comparing alternatives.

Handling Complexity: Manages multiple, often conflicting criteria.

Improved Decision Quality: Enhances transparency and understanding of decision-making.

Conclusion

MCDM is a valuable tool for making complex decisions, supporting informed choices through systematic evaluation.
